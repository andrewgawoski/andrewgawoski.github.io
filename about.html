About Andrew Gawoski

I’m a game designer & developer with over 5 years of independent and academic experience. I had to the good fortune to learn from the meticulous computer science professors and passionate game design professors at the University of California Santa Cruz, where I graduated with a B.Sc. in Computer Science: Computer Game Design (2016). I was given the opportunity to meet and work with some amazing people and teams along the way, picking up solid documentation habits and a small HCI background.

Currently, I'm working on Dawnflower Labyrinth, a game of intimidating (to me) scope and ambition. Of course, that's expected; I'm going to look to make games that only I can make, that challenge me and push me to learn and improve. Recently, that's come in the form of networking as Dawnflower supports networked cooperative play. While I've definitely furthered my understanding of UE4's network replication, I found a lot of questions I didn't know I wanted answered and followed up on them along the way.

I have several hobbies outside the sphere of playing and developing games. I play tennis, am a baseball fan, enjoy cooking, dabble in cryptography, and was a Boy Scout. I have high standards for my own work and love to chase perfection.

While I'm very happy with the work I'm doing now, I still pay attention to new opportunities, particularly to work with larger teams and create games of greater scope.
