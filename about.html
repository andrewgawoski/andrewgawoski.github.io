About

I'm pretty excited you made it to this page. I'm a code-writing, quick-learning UCSC alumnus and SF Bay Area native. I've been making games forever, and programming for at least the last 7 or 8 years. My background in code took off in 2010, working with OOP and C++, where I picked up some fantastic programming habits and fundamentals.

In my spare time I'll watch baseball (Go Giants), play tennis, and occaisionally make some art. When programming, I like to attack problems in a bunch of different ways at once, and I believe in taking the time to write code correctly the first time through. As a student, I've worked on a decent number of games-related group projects, including some from Global Game Jams. While I'm not as satisfied as I could be with many of those titles, I recognize their importance and don't mind talking about them.

Since graduation, I've worked on personal projects like Luna Limit and a much larger 2D action platformer project, tentatively being called Perdix. Independent projects are great to keep my skills sharp and continue learning, but I have actively sought out engineering work at larger studios with varying amounts of success.
