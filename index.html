 Hello, I'm Andrew—
A long journey has brought you here, just as my own has led to the creation of games.
I appreciate your interest in my work.
Please enjoy your visit.

 I've been creating digital games for the better part of the last decade, and have a history of designing tabletop games stretching back nearly twice as long to when I was much younger. Over the years I've had to fill many many unique roles and learn different skillsets, so I pride myself on being able to communicate clearly and establish understanding with just about anyone.

 I recognize that the key to reaching that understanding is learning. So, while I could explain my approach to top-down and bottom-up design or my solid technical background, it's much more important to communicate just how much I love learning. I have a ravenous curiosity and in-depth research completely drives many of my designs. I am at my best when I'm discovering, solving, and approaching new challenges.

 Where to begin? I graduated from the lovely, densely-forested University of California Santa Cruz, receiving a wonderful piece of paper with "Bachelor of Science" and "Computer Science: Computer Game Design" on it. It was definitely a time filled with some strong experiences.

 I prefer tea to coffee, play tennis, watch baseball, and enjoy cooking recreationally. When I'm comfortable, you'll see me making just about as many jokes as I see opportunities for them, a sense of humor keenly honed by my pun-loving friend group growing up and the finest of stand up comedy specials.

 As a game developer, and particularly a designer, I've played an incredible number of games. The only genre I don't particularly enjoy is fighting games, but I can understand why other folks like them. I love talking about design, admittedly with the hopes of a very critical but nuanced and open-ended discussion. Recently, I've been playing a lot of Monster Hunter World, a fantastic cooperative title that I highly recommend, but I'm a little burnt out from. I've also been playing a handful of other titles from a large assortment of different genres, some old, some new, some physical, and some digital.

 I've included several prototypes among my projects in various states of completion. I value the work I've done: heavy design documentation and proof/implementation of core mechanics. I've been fortunate to learn more with every challenge, often carrying those improvements into the next project I work on.

 If you would like to see in-depth design work, I recommend checking out the documentation for Dawnflower Labyrinth (2018). I am currently working on A Fistful of Bones (2018), a western-themed stealth puzzle game.

 I am currently in the process of collecting additional project resources from my past work and modifying this site to include it. This mainly refers to screenshot galleries, but in some cases includes playable prototypes as well.

A stylish 2D action-platformer where players battle through the world with a wind deity's aid to return the sun to the sky. It was developed primarily in C# and targeted gamepad or console usage.

Luna Limit

A single player tile-based game with a strong math influenced developed with Unity. Players aim to create as many full moons as possible by flipping tiles and adding moon phases.

Summoner Timers

A native android application and player assistant for the popular game League of Legends. Summoner Timers automatically populates timers that track various ingame abilities by calling Riot Games' API.

Repository Riot API Gallery

Say La Vie

An educational French-learning game developed in a mid-sized team with UE4 as part of the senior capstone course at UCSC. Players travel to the past to learn words from the French language. The game features voice-acted character dialogue provided by native speakers.
