About Me

Hello there,
 I'M ANDREW
 I make games—I try to make them good ones. In my work, I place a priority on creating understanding. Players need to understand game mechanics, developers need to understand code, and people need to understand eachother. I graduated from the University of California Santa Cruz with a B.Sc in Computer Science: Computer Game Design. My time as a student instilled in me a strong attention to detail and a desire to discover and learn. I've participated in game jams, worked independently, and worked in larger teams of close to a dozen people with diverse backgrounds and ranges of experience. Throughout my time making games, my favorite projects have always questioned whether something was possible and were answered with clever and challenging solutions.

What I'm Working On

Ascengine is an anti-gravity racing title which I've been working on most recently with Unreal Engine 4. Offering a twin-stick control experience and a focus on intuitive, physics-based gameplay, Ascengine aims to bring something fresh to a genre that hasn't seen much change in years.

While I'm pretty proud of the work I've done on Ascengine so far, it's still far from finished. Sporadic updates on the game's progress, including screenshots, design musings, and other development artifacts, are available at the Ascengine development blog.
